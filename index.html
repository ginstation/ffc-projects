(1935-2018)

Isao Takahata

Co-Founder of Studio Ghibli

高畑 勲 (Takahata Isao)

One of the founders of Studio Ghibli alongside Hayao Miyazaki, Toshio Suzuki, and Yasuyoshi Tokuma. Takahata had been long interested in animations since watching the film, "Le Roi et l'Oiseau" (The King and the Mockingbird). He previously worked within other animation studios such as Toei Animation, Nippon Animation, and Telecom Animation Film Co. Ltd as an animations director and producer. While in Toei he met Hayao Miyazaki, who joined his team in one of Takahata's first works, "Hols: Prince of the Sun." Although the film was a commerical failure (but now one of Japan's first defining works in modern animation), the two continued to collaborate on several other projects before Studio Ghibli's creation.

Timeline

1935 Born October 29 in Ise (formerly Ujiyamada) of the Mie Prefecture in Japan. Takahata was the youngest of 7 siblings.
1945 Survived a major US air raid in Okayama.
1959 Graduated in University of Tokyo with a degree in French literature.
1961 Started some time ago as an assistant director under Yasuo Otsuka in Toei Animation. Takahata helped direct several animated films and tv shows such as the anime "Ken the Wolf Boy (1963)"
1968 Directorial debut in the feature film "Hols: Prince of the Sun." Hayao Miyazaki was also a part of the team as a key animator.
1971 Left Toei Animation alongside Miyazaki and Yoichi Kotabe (animator and character designer).
1974 Worked alongside Miyazaki and Kotabe to create the anime series "Heidi, Girl of the Alps" for Zuiyo Enterprise (the animation production side later became Nippon Animation).
1981 Left Nippon and joined Telecom Animation Film Co. Ltd.
1985 Officially formed Studio Ghibli alongside Miyazaki and Miyazaki's collaborators, Toshio Suzuki and Yasuyoshi Tokuma.
1988 Directed his first Ghibli film, "Grave of the Fireflies." Continued to direct films for Studio Ghibli, with the exception of "Kiki's Delivery Service" (1989) as its musical director.
2013 Directed his last film "The Tale of Princess Kaguya."
2018 Passed away in a hospital in Tokyo on April 5 due to lung cancer.
